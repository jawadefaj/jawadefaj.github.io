I am a Ph.D. candidate in the computational media at the University of California Santa Cruz , specializing in human driver behavior modeling for autonomous vehicle testing. My educational journey started with a bachelor's degree in computer science and engineering from the Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology, then I went on to earn a master's in computational media at UCSC.
My passion for game development led me to co-found a game studio in Bangladesh during my sophomore year, where I developed "Heroes of 71," a game that received huge attention due to its cultural significance. At UCSC, I expanded my expertise into game engine technology, contributing to a Ford-funded project on synthetic road network generation for AV simulation.
My technical skill set spans across programming languages like Python, C++, and JavaScript, and extends to data analysis, visualization, and machine learning. I am also an active participant in ITS and ADAS conferences and take pride in mentoring students through UCSC's summer internship program, combining my love for chess and commitment to teaching.
Here's what you'll find on this website. Under the Research tab, I maintain a list of my publications and research projects. On the Projects tab, you can find a bunch of my other projects. Please find my contact information below this page, as well as other links. To see all my public project repositories, check out my GitHub profile.
