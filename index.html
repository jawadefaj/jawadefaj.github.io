My name is Abdul Jawad, and I am a Ph.D. candidate at the University of California Santa Cruz . My research involves modeling human driving behavior to assist simulation-based autonomous vehicle testing. My goal is to accelerate the development and deployment of autonomous vehicles by improving their testing process through advanced simulation techniques. In addition to publishing at academic conferences, I have contributed to open-source projects related to AV simulation tool development. The tools are primarily developed in Python and C++. I have been using Unreal and Unity game engines for seven years due to my exposure to game development. I am primarily interested in modeling behaviors for simulation-based AV testing for my dissertation project.

I received my BSc in Computer Science and Engineering from Bangladesh. I co-founded a game studio and published four Andriod games during my undergraduate. In 2018, I moved to the University of California, Santa Cruz, to pursue a PhD in the Computational Media Department.

Here's what you'll find on this website. Under the Research tab, I maintain a list of my publications and research projects. On the Projects tab, you can find a bunch of my other projects. Please find my contact information below this page, as well as other links. To see all my public project repositories, check out my GitHub profile.
